# Polar coordinates are beautiful

Just go over to ${partials.linkExternal "https://www.desmos.com/calculator" "desmos"} and type a random equation with polar coordinates. There is a very good chance, you see something visually interesting.

Examples (try with $-12\pi\le\theta\le12\pi$):
- $r=\theta$: the simplest form is already a spiral
- $r=\theta^{2}$: sqaure it, something that looks like infinite hear shapes
- $r=e^{\theta}$: try zooming into it, it goes on forever
- $r=\sin\left(0.3\theta\right)$: some floral shape. mess around with the factor (0.3) to watch it unravel in really cool ways.
- $r=e^{\theta\left(\sin\left(\theta\right)\cos\left(\theta\right)\right)^{6}}$: would make a really cool visualizer for music

Designing a logo? Just play around with polar coordinates till you find a shape inside it that looks interesting. Won't take long.
